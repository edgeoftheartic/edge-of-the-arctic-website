What's nearby

Wonders within reach

North and Northeast Iceland sit along the Arctic Coast Way, where the plate boundaries of two continents meet the open Arctic Ocean. Fewer crowds, bigger skies — midnight sun in summer, northern lights in winter.

On the doorstep

Eyjafjörður & Akureyri

Whale watching on the calm fjord, geothermal baths, the Forest Lagoon, botanical gardens and North Iceland's capital — all within twenty minutes.

Under an hour

Goðafoss

The "waterfall of the gods" — a broad, beautiful horseshoe falls on the road east, and one of Iceland's most photographed.

Day trip

Dettifoss & Jökulsárgljúfur

Europe's most powerful waterfall thunders through a 25 km canyon of volcanic rock formations and babbling springbrooks.

Day trip

Ásbyrgi

A vast horseshoe of sheer cliffs and birch forest — in folklore, the hoofprint of Odin's eight-legged horse. Magical in every season.

The far northeast

The Arctic Henge & Raufarhöfn

At Iceland's northernmost village, a monumental stone circle built on mythology and the midnight sun, just short of the Arctic Circle.

For the adventurous

Langanes & the northeast coast

Gannet colonies, a ghost village, and the wild finger of Langanes reaching into the Arctic Ocean — the Iceland almost nobody reaches.
