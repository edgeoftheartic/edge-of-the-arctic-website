The place

A village most travellers drive past

Hjalteyri is a former herring station on the western shore of Eyjafjörður — a handful of houses, a historic factory turned art space, seals in the shallows and mountains across the water. It's the kind of place people mean when they say "the real Iceland."

Staying with us puts you at the centre of the north: Akureyri in twenty minutes, Goðafoss in under an hour, whale watching from the fjord, and the wild northeast coast within a day's drive.

Guests on our expeditions stay here as part of their journey — but the rooms are yours to book for any independent trip north.

Good to know

The Essentials

Rooms

Comfortable double and twin rooms with fjord or mountain views, shared guest kitchen and lounge. Breakfast available by arrangement.

Dinner is sorted

Eyri Restaurant is a two-minute walk. Guests staying with us always get a table — ask us to reserve when you book your room.

Getting here

19 km north of Akureyri (20 minutes by car). Akureyri airport is a 45-minute domestic flight from Reykjavík. Airport pickup available on request.

Rates and room details are confirmed by email — tell us your dates and party size and we'll reply within 24 hours with availability and a price.
