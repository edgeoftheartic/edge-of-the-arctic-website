Why Reset Croatia

Croatia is best understood slowly

Reset Croatia is designed for travellers who want more than a checklist. The journey moves through the city, the land and the sea — but it does so with time to pause, speak with local people, taste regional food, and feel the rhythm of each place.

The heart of the journey is the Foscari Estate: a restored heritage stone home in the Istrian countryside where mornings begin slowly, afternoons lead into vineyards and hill towns, and evenings end with wine beneath the courtyard lights. Then the sea takes over — five nights aboard M/S Stella Maris, a traditional gulet, waking in a new harbour each day.

Every included activity is optional. Join the excursions, cycle, rest, book a massage, read in the courtyard or linger on deck. Reset Croatia is enriching, never exhausting.

Nights 2–6 · Istria

Foscari Estate

Five nights in a restored stone estate — the calm centre of the Istrian chapter. Hum and the Aura distillery, Motovun, truffle hunting with the Karlić family's dogs, a pasta and gnocchi workshop, two wineries, and dinner at the 1908 Stancija farmstead.

Nights 7–11 · Dalmatia

M/S Stella Maris

A floating boutique home from Zadar to Trogir. Breakfast and dinner aboard, selected evenings ashore in konobas, e-bikes throughout the cruise, and island days on Vis, Korčula and Hvar.

Throughout

The living Adriatic

This is not only about beautiful islands — it's about how the Adriatic shaped daily life: fishing, wine, olives, stone towns, migration and trade. The fishing boat, the gulet and the harbour all become part of the same story.

Day by day

The Itinerary

  1. Day 1 · Zagreb

    Elegant arrival

    Arrive in Zagreb and settle into Le Premier Hotel. At 18:00, join a two-hour Secret Zagreb walking tour through hidden courtyards, stories and local legends.

  2. Day 2 · Into Istria

    Hum, the smallest city in the world

    Depart for Istria, stopping in Hum to visit the Aura Distillery Museum — herbs, fruit and regional tradition. Continue to the Foscari Estate for five nights, with Morosini-Grimani Castle and dinner at Restaurant 53.

  3. Day 3 · Motovun

    Truffles

    Morning walk in hilltop Motovun, then truffle hunting with the Karlić family and their dogs, followed by lunch and a hands-on gnocchi and pasta-making workshop.

  4. Day 4 · The coast

    Rovinj, Brijuni & the Lim Canal

    A coastal day shaped by seaside beauty: a Rovinj walking tour, Brijuni National Park and a cruise along the Lim Canal. Dinner at Viking Restaurant.

  5. Day 5 · Flavours

    Wine & olive oil

    Tastings with two wineries and local producers, ending with a home-style dinner at Stancija 1908, an old Istrian farmstead.

  6. Day 6 · Pula

    The Roman Arena

    Visit Pula and its Roman amphitheatre. The afternoon and evening are free — rest, explore independently, or dine on your own.

  7. Day 7 · To the sea

    Board M/S Stella Maris

    Drive toward Dalmatia with a lunch stop in Opatija. In Zadar, board the Stella Maris and enjoy a guided city tour before your first night aboard.

  8. Day 8 · Šibenik & Krka

    Waterfalls and stone streets

    Visit Šibenik and continue to Krka National Park, returning to the Stella Maris for dinner and a relaxed evening on deck.

  9. Day 9 · Vis

    The authentic island

    Explore one of Croatia's most unspoiled islands — walk, ride an e-bike, swim, or enjoy the island pace from the harbour and deck.

  10. Day 10 · Korčula

    Medieval streets and wine

    Discover Korčula's medieval old town, its wine traditions and island culture.

  11. Day 11 · Hvar

    Stari Grad

    Explore Stari Grad and the UNESCO-listed Stari Grad Plain — vineyards, olive groves and optional e-bike routes.

  12. Day 12 · Split & Trogir

    Roman Dalmatia, farewell

    End with Roman Dalmatia and the stone beauty of Trogir before the journey's farewell.
