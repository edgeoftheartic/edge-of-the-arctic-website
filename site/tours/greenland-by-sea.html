Why this journey is different

Very few travellers ever see Greenland this way

Most visitors to Greenland see one town and fly home. On this expedition you experience the country the way Greenlanders do — travelling its spectacular southwest coast by sea aboard the Amarok, an overnight coastal voyage almost no international visitor ever makes.

You'll explore the colourful capital Nuuk, settle into charming Qaqortoq, and spend two nights at the remote Tasermiut Camp beneath some of the greatest granite walls on Earth — before flying to Iceland to finish with whale watching, geothermal baths and exceptional food around Akureyri and Eyjafjörður, hosted at our own restaurant and guesthouse.

Every departure is personally led by your hosts, Nik and Sarah, with logistics that would be nearly impossible to arrange independently.

Day by day

The Itinerary

  1. Days 1–3 · Nuuk

    Greenland's colourful capital

    Arrive in Nuuk and settle in with a welcome dinner. Two full days exploring the old colonial harbour, the Greenland National Museum, and the fjordlands around the capital — with time to meet the city on your own terms.

  2. Days 3–5 · Aboard the Amarok

    The coastal voyage south

    Board the Amarok for the overnight voyage down Greenland's southwest coast — a working coastal route travelled by Greenlanders, not tourists. Watch icebergs, villages and mountains slide past from the deck. Cabins reserved for our group.

  3. Days 5–8 · Qaqortoq

    Three nights in South Greenland's prettiest town

    Qaqortoq's brightly painted houses climb the hills above the harbour. From here we explore Norse ruins, hot springs at Uunartoq, and the Stone & Man open-air sculpture trail.

  4. Days 8–10 · Tasermiut Camp

    Two nights beneath the great walls

    By boat into Tasermiut Fjord — one of the most dramatic places in the Arctic, ringed by granite faces that draw the world's best climbers. Comfortable camp, extraordinary silence. Almost nobody has heard of it before they book; nobody forgets it after.

  5. Day 11 · Flight to Iceland

    Across the Denmark Strait

    Fly from South Greenland to Iceland and connect north to Akureyri, the capital of North Iceland, at the head of Eyjafjörður fjord.

  6. Days 11–14 · Akureyri & Eyjafjörður

    The warm finish: North Iceland

    Three nights around Akureyri as our personal guests — whale watching on the fjord, geothermal baths, Goðafoss waterfall, and dinner at our own Eyri Restaurant on the shore at Hjalteyri. Depart from Akureyri or Keflavík.
