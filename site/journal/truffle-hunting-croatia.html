Truffle Hunting in Croatia

For a food lover there is no greater thrill than finding an ingredient at its source. Truffle hunting in Croatia is a raw little adventure in the Mediterranean forest — guided by expert eyes and extraordinary dogs — and it ends, as all good adventures should, at the table.

Where the diamonds grow

  • Buzet & Motovun: the heart of the truffle world — hilltop towns, dense oak forest and castles perched above the Mirna valley.
  • Livade: official home of the autumn truffle festival, where the whole community celebrates the harvest.
  • The coast nearby: Rovinj and Poreč blend the hunt with salty Adriatic air.

The seasons

Black truffles are found much of the year, peaking from September to December. White truffles — the world's most prized — run a short season from late September to year's end. That window is the ultimate highlight of our Istrian journeys.

The dogs do the finding

The art relies on the bond between hunter and dog — often the Lagotto Romagnolo, whose nose is legendary. You'll watch the signal, the careful dig that protects the forest floor, and learn why certain soils and trees hide more treasure than others.

Then, the eating

Guests tell us the highlight is the meal after the hunt: fresh truffle shaved over handmade pasta or eggs, hours out of the earth, with aged Istrian wine alongside. The hunt is part of our Reset Croatia journey and can anchor any private Istrian itinerary — sustainability, local pride and lunch, all in one morning.
