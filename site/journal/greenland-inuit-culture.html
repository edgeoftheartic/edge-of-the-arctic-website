Greenland's Inuit Culture: Traditions of the Arctic

Greenland is a place where time slows down, letting the rich history and soul of the Inuit people take centre stage. Travelling here is not sightseeing — it is an invitation to understand the Arctic through the eyes of those who call it home.

Direct contact, real exchange

Deep cultural traditions are woven into daily life. We prioritise genuine meetings with local people — in Nuuk, Ilulissat and Sisimiut — where our local partners share both history and modern life, including a culinary introduction to Greenlandic food built on reindeer and fresh fish.

What you experience

  • Culinary traditions: sea-to-table dishes and their significance in Inuit survival.
  • Art and performance: traditional drum dancing and storytelling, where every beat carries the weight of Arctic history.
  • Handmade heritage: artisans creating traditional tools and clothing — the skills that make life possible at the edge of the world.

Places and rhythms

Nuuk tells the national story with small-town charm; Ilulissat's icefjord walks pass beneath shifting cathedral-sized bergs; Sisimiut hums with traditional crafts and modern Inuit life. June to September brings the long days and milder weather best suited to full immersion.

Art as identity

Inuit art preserves a legacy: stone carvings that echo Arctic wildlife, textiles whose patterns tell stories of hunt and family, tools of bone and skin that are both functional and beautiful. Meeting the artists changes how you see every piece.

We travel exclusively with local providers, in small groups, so the communities we visit benefit from every journey. Ice, fire and unconditional hospitality — Greenland gives all three.
