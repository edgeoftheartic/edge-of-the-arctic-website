Hidden Gems of Bulgaria

Bulgaria hides extraordinary stories in places most tourists never reach — from the picturesque village of Zheravna, with its traditional architecture, to wine-centric Melnik among ancient ruins. Here is why this Balkan country deserves your curiosity.

Treasures off the tourist routes

Zheravna, nestled in the mountains, captivates with 18th-century wooden houses and narrow lanes made for slow mornings. Melnik — Bulgaria's smallest town — is a sanctuary for wine lovers, set against surreal sandstone pyramids, where you taste local wines and the ancient viticulture that defines the region.

Experiences you join, not watch

Annual festivals in the Balkan mountains serve regional specialities that haven't changed in centuries — ancient rituals, local families, and a seat at their tables. As a chef-led company we lean into the food: learn to make banitsa or shopska salad with local experts, and expect to be welcomed into homes where hospitality is unmatched.

Trails without the crowds

  • Krushuna Waterfalls — easy trails through a hidden oasis of cascades in the north.
  • Mount Musala — the highest peak in the Balkans, in the Rila range.
  • The Strandzha mountains — quiet, pristine and blissfully unknown.

Rila and Pirin national parks offer world-class trekking for every level, and the Vitosha region near Sofia rewards cyclists.

Travelling it well

Choose the smaller towns — Zheravna, Koprivshtitsa — and sleep in cosy guesthouses. Eat in family-run mehanas (traditional taverns). Say yes to the village festival. Bulgaria is Europe's best-kept secret precisely because it stayed authentic; we run it as private, custom journeys — tell us your dates.
