Spring in Iceland: the Awakening North

Spring in Iceland is a season of transformation: the days stretch longer with every sunset, the snow releases the valleys, and the island fills with returning life. From March to May you get an Iceland far more intimate than the busy summer — and we think it might be the best time to come.

A season with everything

Expect sun, rain and snow in one afternoon — temperatures from 0 to 10°C, and up to nineteen hours of daylight by May. The unpredictability is part of the adventure; the logistics and safety are our job.

The culture blooms too

Sumardagurinn fyrsti — the First Day of Summer — arrives in late April with parades and unreasonable optimism. Music festivals, Easter markets and small-town celebrations put you shoulder to shoulder with locals at their happiest.

Lambs and puffins

This is the island at its most alive. Puffins return to the cliffs in late April; the waters off Húsavík and Akureyri wake with migrating whales. And on the farms, lambing season begins — our farm visits let you watch newborn lambs take their first steps, a quietly moving experience very few travellers ever see.

Why go with us

Rugged transport for the shoulder-season roads, itineraries that flex with the forecast, and farm and festival access that comes from living here. Our Vibrant Iceland in Spring journey (next departure May 2027) is built around exactly this season — birds, lambs, waterfalls in full thunder and long bright evenings.
