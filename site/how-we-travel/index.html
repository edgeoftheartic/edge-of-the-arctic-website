Our commitment

Designing journeys that respect places, people and culture

At Edge of the Arctic and Beyond, we believe travel should benefit the places and people we visit. Our philosophy is rooted in meaningful, small-scale experiences that support local communities, protect nature, and encourage cultural connection.

We intentionally stay away from mass tourism and overcrowded destinations whenever possible. Instead we focus on authentic experiences, lesser-known regions and the countryside — places where tourism can have a more positive and sustainable impact.

Across Iceland, the Balkans, the Faroe Islands, Greenland and everywhere we operate, we prioritise family-run accommodations, small boutique hotels and guesthouses, local farms, independent restaurants, small wineries and regional producers, and local guides and artisans. We choose not to work with large international hotel chains whenever possible — locally owned businesses create a more authentic experience while keeping tourism revenue in the community.

Slower is better

Small groups, big impact

Our tours are designed for slower, more thoughtful travel. We often stay multiple nights in one place to reduce unnecessary transport and let guests connect more deeply with local culture and environment.

We travel light — usually 6–16 guests in nine-passenger vans that reach hidden villages and remote corners larger buses never can. Small group sizes are central to our philosophy: less impact, more meaning.

For us, responsible travel is not a marketing trend — it is the foundation of how we design every journey. We are committed to continually improving our sustainability practices and working with partners who share these values.

The path behind our philosophy

Rooted in the North, defined by local insight

Our story begins in the remote, wild northeast corner of Iceland — where our journey in hospitality and travel truly began, with a small restaurant and guesthouse.

As tourism in Iceland grew, larger chains and big tour companies arrived with it — and that made us value the personal side of travel all the more. We began to imagine something smaller, more genuine, closer to the places and people that make each destination special.

That idea became what we do today: journeys where travellers meet local people, taste authentic food and experience culture up close. As guests returned year after year, our multilingual family of staff — connected by personal ties to the regions we visit — helped us grow organically beyond Iceland into Croatia, Greece, Bosnia, Slovenia and Bulgaria, keeping the same warm, immersive approach travellers love.
