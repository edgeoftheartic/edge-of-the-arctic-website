The wonders of the north

Places we'll take you — or point you toward

North and Northeast Iceland sit along the Arctic Coast Way, where the plate boundaries of North America and Eurasia meet the open Arctic Ocean. Fewer crowds, bigger skies, midnight sun in summer and northern lights in winter.

Our base

Akureyri & Eyjafjörður

The capital of North Iceland, at the head of the country's longest fjord. Whale watching, geothermal baths, botanical gardens, great coffee — and Hjalteyri, our home village, twenty minutes up the shore.

Vatnajökull National Park

Dettifoss & Jökulsárgljúfur

Europe's most powerful waterfall thunders through a 25 km canyon carved by catastrophic glacial floods — with the babbling springbrooks of Hólmatungur and the volcanic rock formations of Hljóðaklettar nearby.

Nature pearl

Ásbyrgi

A vast horseshoe-shaped enclosure of sheer cliffs and birch forest — in folklore, the hoofprint of Odin's eight-legged horse. Magical in every season, with a fine network of hiking trails.

Arctic Circle village

Raufarhöfn & the Arctic Henge

At Iceland's northernmost village, a monumental stone circle built on mythology and the midnight sun. Just offshore lies the Arctic Circle itself — front-row seats for summer's endless light and winter's aurora.

Birdlife & coastal culture

Langanes Peninsula

A 40 km finger of land reaching into the Arctic Ocean: gannet colonies at Skoruvíkurbjarg, the ghost village of Skálar, and the fishing town Þórshöfn — where our story began with a small guesthouse and restaurant.

For the birders

Melrakkaslétta & the Birding Trail

Driftwood shores, lakes and lagoons, seals on the skerries and some of Iceland's richest birdlife. Rauðinúpur cape — an ice-age volcano capped with a lighthouse — rewards a rocky hike with huge views.
